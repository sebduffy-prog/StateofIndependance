The twists in the story

There are twists in the story.

Beneath the expected cutbacks, three anomalies emerge. This is not a population retreating into a bunker — it is rewriting the rules of survival, institutional trust and technology.

Twist one

The trust paradox.

Banks are no longer the enemy — even though confidence is slipping. Drag the seven institutions into the order you think Britain trusts to be there over the next twelve months, most trusted at the top.

Source: VCCP x Watermelon Research COCL survey · 1,504 UK adults 18+, nat. rep. · May 2026. (Q7 % rating 7–10; Q7 mean; Q8C % declined, exact 52.6.)

Twist two

Protected joy.

The lipstick effect is real. Hospitality and fashion are vulnerable — holidays remain sacrosanct, reclassified as a wellbeing line item to defend, not a luxury to trim.

Protecting the holiday budget are protecting their holiday budget at all costs.

Source: VCCP x Watermelon Research COCL survey · 1,504 UK adults 18+, nat. rep. · May 2026. (Q5 % protecting.)

Twist three

AI on tap.

Expertise once gatekept by professionals is now a prompt away — substituted across finance, health and legal advice.

Used AI instead of a professional — any task for at least one task in the last six months.
High-stakes only — finance, health or legal used AI for at least one high-stakes call.

Source: VCCP x Watermelon Research COCL survey · 1,504 UK adults 18+, nat. rep. · May 2026. (Q11; any task exact 58.4, high-stakes exact 37.4.)
