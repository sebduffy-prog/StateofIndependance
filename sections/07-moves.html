The playbook

Five signature moves

The playbook for the age of independence: five shifts in how brands behave, each moving from the old model of dependence to the new model of agency.

  1. 01 Unplug them from the grid
  2. 02 Be the trusted antidote

01

Unplug them from the grid

Find practical, everyday ways to help your customers lower their living costs and rely less on broken systems.

34% are actively using tools to fix their practical home and financial problems themselves.
  • At the mercy of inflation
  • At the mercy of the energy crisis
  • At the mercy of government policy

I now have a lot less trust in institutions such as the government and politicians, local councils, than I did a few years ago… there's just scandal after scandal. You know, they're in it for themselves… Big brands are the same — I don't want to give too much money to one brand.

Southampton, 63, Architects

Be the trusted antidote

So I'd probably do half their job for them before actually calling them.

03

Ride the social self-help wave

People are teaching each other to fix, save and do it themselves. Meet them in the self-help contexts where that energy already lives.

The nation's self-help manuals live on platforms where context is king — not targeting people, but targeting the “self-help” behaviour through contexts and content.

I do use ChatGPT all the time and find it really quite helpful when I do have problems… I do feel more empowered now with everything at my fingertips and I feel as if I've got more free time because of that.

Wales, 68, Architects

Whether it's fixing things with YouTube videos or going on Money Saving Expert looking for the best deals, shopping around, going on Instagram for hacks.

Bury, 39, Hustler

04

Kill the mental load

The modern challenge is cognitive overload, and the sandwich generation carries the heaviest load of all.

  • Death to ‘I'm too busy’
  • Death to ‘It's confusing’
  • Death to ‘I'm stressed’
  • 87% have children they support financially
  • 58% support their partners
  • 31% support their parents financially
  • 12% support younger extended family members

The brand I find most aspirational isn't a fashion house, it's Cook or Mindful Chef. Time is the rarest thing I have. Paying someone to solve dinner is the ultimate luxury.
