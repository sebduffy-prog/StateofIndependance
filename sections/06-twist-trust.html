The trust paradox

Seven institutions. Rank them as you think Britain does — most trusted to least. Then meet the order the country actually gave.

What Britain said

53% to 24%

Confidence runs from the NHS at the top to government at the bottom — a 29-point spread, and only one institution of seven clears half the country.

The NHS scores 6.42 / 10 — the most trusted institution in Britain. Yet 53% say its performance has declined over the past decade. High regard and falling confidence, side by side.
