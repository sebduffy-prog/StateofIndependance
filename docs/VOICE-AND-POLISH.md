# VOICE-AND-POLISH.md — copy voice + interaction + frontend cheat-sheets

Reference for the copy/polish agent working on **The State of Independence**. Four
cheat-sheets: (A) WLV voice, (B) em-dash purge, (C) click-path audit, (D) frontend
top-tier. Be concrete; follow the patterns; never change a verified stat or a verbatim quote.

Source of truth for copy + data: `docs/STORY.md`. **Deck wins on conflict.** Every stat
must stay identical to STORY.md — only voice and punctuation change.

---

## A) WLV VOICE CHEAT-SHEET

**WLV = "Write Like Vallance"** — the VCCP house thought-leadership voice (Charles
Vallance, chairman + founding partner). Erudite worn lightly, dry British wit, one frame
carried through, real attributed evidence, an optimist's turn at the end. On a data site,
we are not writing 650-word columns — we are applying the *register* to headlines,
standfirsts, stat callouts, kickers and buttons.

### The five rules that matter here

1. **Hook, never throat-clear.** Open on tension or a vivid claim, not a label.
   Don't: "This section looks at consumer behaviour." Do: "Britain has stopped waiting
   to be rescued."
2. **One frame per surface.** Each chapter rides a single metaphor/idea (a baseline, a
   twist, an architecture, a set of moves). Don't stack three metaphors in one standfirst.
3. **Real evidence, named, unchanged.** Every number traces to STORY.md. Name the source
   in prose the way Charles does ("a COCL survey of 1,504 adults found…"). Never round,
   never invent, never soften a stat to fit the rhythm.
4. **Turn optimistic.** Even on anxiety or distrust, pivot to agency. The data is not a
   bunker; it is a population taking back control. That is the whole site's thesis — keep
   it in the voice.
5. **Witty, dry kicker.** Land closes with a smile, a callback, an aside. Never an
   exclamation mark (vanishingly rare). Never agency cliché.

### Sentence rhythm

- Short paragraphs, one to three sentences. White space is part of the argument.
- **Vary length deliberately.** Punchy short line for emphasis; one longer clause-rich
  line to build. The reader should *hear* it.
- Tricolons land hard: "Organised. Positive. In control." Keep the segment trios exactly
  as STORY.md has them — they are already perfect WLV rhythm.
- British spelling and idiom throughout (behaviour, recognise, defence).
- Rhetorical question to pivot is fine, sparingly.

### Vocabulary — do / don't

**Do:** plain strong nouns and verbs; coin a memorable term when it earns the frame
(STORY.md already has "the empowerment architecture", "signature moves", "the new
baseline" — lean on these). Confidence softened by modesty.

**Don't (banned agency cliché):** unlock, disrupt, leverage, game-changing, synergy,
"in today's fast-paced world", "consumers" (prefer "people" / "Britain" / "the nation"),
deep-dive, journey, seamless, supercharge, empower (as a verb-tic — the *idea* of
empowerment is fine; the buzzword is not). No exclamation marks. No emoji.

### How to write each surface type

| Surface | Job | WLV rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Kicker** (above headline) | Orient in 2–4 words | Label-ish is OK here. Sentence case. e.g. "The numbers you already know". |
| **Headline** | The frame, one line, sentence case | ONE highlighter word in `*asterisks*` (STORY.md convention). Max two highlighter motifs per surface. A claim, not a topic. |
| **Standfirst** | Set tension, hand off to the data | 1–3 short sentences. Hook then turn. No em dashes (see §B). Keep meaning + char-count feel close to STORY.md. |
| **Stat callout** | One number, made to mean something | Lead with the number, then a plain-English "so what". Don't explain twice. "77% are more careful with money than five years ago. Carefulness is now the default lens, not a phase." |
| **Button / CTA** | One clear action | Verb-led, 1–4 words, sentence case. "Explore the data", "See the four Britains", "Rank them yourself". Avoid "Click here", "Learn more", "Discover". |
| **Section kicker → headline pair** | Together carry the chapter | Kicker = where we are; headline = the twist. Don't repeat words across the two. |

turn → stop. Dashes become full stops, colons or commas. Stats and quotes untouched.

## B) EM-DASH PURGE RULES

Hard constraint: **no em dashes (—) anywhere in copy.** En dashes (–) used as a
sentence dash are out too. Replace, don't just delete. **Verbatim research quotes are
exempt** — quoted speech stays exactly as recorded (see exceptions below).

### The replacement decision tree

For each `—` (or dash-used `–`), pick the replacement that fits the grammar:

1. **Two independent clauses, second explains/expands the first → colon.**
   "the operational floor — table stakes" → "the operational floor: table stakes".
2. **Sharp break / new sentence works → full stop.**
   "into a bunker — it is rewriting…" → "into a bunker. It is rewriting…".
3. **Parenthetical aside → commas (or parentheses if it's a true aside).**
   "this time we dug deeper — beyond how people feel — into what…" →
   "this time we dug deeper, past how people feel, into what…".
4. **List glue / "and" was implied → comma or "and".**
   "survival, institutional trust and technology" stays; only the lead-in dash changes.
5. **Number / date / score RANGE → "to".** This is the key one for this data site:
   - "53% → 24%" or "53% — 24%" → "from 53% to 24%" (or "53% to 24%").
   - "71 years — 62 years vs 2 months": keep as written prose; if a dash joins a range,
     use "to". The arrow `→` in tables/visuals is a visual element, not copy — leave arrows
     in chart labels and "Less → More" tables alone; only purge dashes in sentence prose.
   - "0.26–0.53" timecodes inside quote attributions: these live in *quotes/attributions*,
     leave untouched (they are verbatim source refs).

### What to PRESERVE

- **Hyphens stay** (cost-conscious, self-reliant, own-label, 18+, mid-May, week-long,
  full-funnel). A hyphen joining a compound word is not a dash. Do not touch these.
- **Number ranges** read as "X to Y", never "X–Y" in prose.
- **Verbatim quotes** — every block quote attributed to a person, city, or "qualitative
  research" / "video diaries" / Martin Lewis etc. is recorded speech. If a transcribed
  quote contains a dash as the person spoke/as transcribed, **leave it**. STORY.md says
  "Quotes are verbatim — do not tidy grammar." That includes their dashes.
- **Arrows `→` and "Less / More", "From / To" table structures** are visual/data, not
  prose. Leave them.

### Worked examples

| Before (with dash) | After (no dash) | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| "taking back control — and what it demands" | "taking back control, and what it demands" | comma (4) |
| "operational floor — table stakes, not the strategy" | "operational floor: table stakes, not the strategy" | colon (1) |
| "into a bunker — it is rewriting" | "into a bunker. It is rewriting" | full stop (2) |
| "53% → 24% — a 29-point spread" | "from 53% to 24%. A 29-point spread" | range "to" + full stop (5,2) |
| "we dug deeper — beyond how people feel, into…" | "we dug deeper: past how people feel, into…" | colon (1) |
| "Numbers for the what — real lives for the why" | "Numbers for the what. Real lives for the why." | full stop (2) |
| "self-reliant" / "cost-conscious" | unchanged | hyphen, preserve |
| Quote: "…tax codes and self-assessment — by and large…" (verbatim) | unchanged | verbatim exempt |

### How to sweep

Search files for the literal characters and review each in context (do **not** blanket
replace, because of ranges, hyphens and quotes):

```
grep -rn "—" sections/ js/sections/ js/main.js js/lib/ docs/STORY.md
grep -rn "–" sections/ js/sections/ js/main.js js/lib/ docs/STORY.md   # en dash as dash
```

For each hit: is it inside a verbatim quote? leave it. Is it a number range? "to". Else
apply the decision tree. Re-grep after to confirm only quote/range cases remain.

## C) CLICK-PATH AUDIT CHECKLIST

Trace every interactive touchpoint through its full state-change sequence. The bug class
this catches: handlers that each work alone but cancel each other, leave wrong final
state, or break the path. Method per touchpoint: identify handler → trace each call in
order → for each, what does it read/write and does it RESET shared state as a side effect
→ does a later call undo an earlier one → is the FINAL state what the control promises.

First build a side-effect map of any shared state (scroll position, active section,
quiz answers, selected segment, active filter). Note any setter that resets fields it
does not own. That map is the reference for every check below.

### Per-interaction checklist

**1. Scroll cue (cover → ch.02)**
- [ ] Cue is visible on load, animates subtly, and points down.
- [ ] Click/keyboard on the cue scrolls to the next section (not just decorative).
- [ ] Cue hides or fades once the user has scrolled past the cover (doesn't linger).
- Common breakage: cue is a div with no handler (dead), or hidden behind a z-index layer.
- A11y: focusable, has an accessible label ("Scroll to begin"), reachable by keyboard.

**2. Nav (chapter jumps / progress)**
- [ ] Every nav item scrolls to the correct section and there is a 1:1 match to 9 chapters.
- [ ] Active state updates as you scroll (IntersectionObserver) AND when you click.
- [ ] Clicking nav doesn't fight the scroll observer (clicking ch.05 then the observer
      snapping active back to ch.04 = Sequential Undo / observer interference).
- Common breakage: click sets active, scroll handler immediately overwrites it; smooth
  scroll + observer race produces a flickering active item.
- A11y: nav is a `<nav>`, items are buttons/links, current item has `aria-current`,
  focus visible.

**3. Quiz (which segment are you?)**
- [ ] Each answer registers and advances; selected answer shows selected state.
- [ ] Final state computes the right segment from all answers (not just the last one —
      check it isn't a stale-closure bug capturing one answer).
- [ ] "Restart" / back fully clears prior answers (doesn't leave a half-reset that skews
      the result).
- [ ] Result maps to deck segment names + sizes (Architects 17 / Hustlers 28 / Coasters 27
      / Retreaters 28), never the old crosstab labels.
- Common breakage: result shows before all questions answered; restart resets the view but
  not the answers array; clicking the same option twice toggles it off and breaks scoring.
- A11y: options are real radio/button controls, keyboard operable, result announced
  (`aria-live`).

**4. Drag-rank (rank the items)**
- [ ] Items reorder on drag and the new order persists in state.
- [ ] Keyboard alternative exists (move up/down buttons or arrow keys) — drag-only fails a11y.
- [ ] Drop outside the list doesn't lose an item or duplicate it.
- [ ] Any "check / reveal" reads the CURRENT order, not the initial order (stale state).
- Common breakage: order updates visually via DOM but state array isn't updated, so the
  reveal scores the original order; touch devices can't drag.
- A11y: each item focusable, instructions present, screen-reader move announcements.

**5. Segment map / 2x2 (the four Britains)**
- [ ] Clicking a quadrant selects that segment and shows its panel; only one active at a time.
- [ ] Selecting segment B clears segment A's panel (no two panels stuck open).
- [ ] Axis labels (pessimistic↔optimistic, passive↔proactive) render and the segment sits
      in the correct quadrant per STORY.md.
- [ ] Sizes shown are deck sizes 17/28/27/28; survey n only as small-print.
- Common breakage: select handler sets new segment but doesn't clear prior selected styling;
  map and detail panel read different state sources and disagree.
- A11y: quadrants are buttons with labels, keyboard selectable, selected state not
  colour-only.

**6. Pillars (Save MONEY / TIME / STRESS)**
- [ ] Each pillar expands/reveals its content; collapsing one when opening another (if
      accordion) actually closes the prior one.
- [ ] If all-open is intended, opening one must not close another (check the toggle logic).
- Common breakage: accordion sets openIndex but a second setter resets it; "open all"
  and single-toggle logic conflict.
- A11y: `aria-expanded` on the trigger, content associated via `aria-controls`, keyboard
  toggle.

**7. Flip rows (Less → More shift tables)**
- [ ] Hover/click flips the row to reveal the "More" side; flips back cleanly.
- [ ] Flipped state is per-row, not global (flipping row 1 must not flip rows 2–3).
- [ ] On touch, there is a tap path (hover-only fails on mobile).
- Common breakage: shared `isFlipped` flag flips all rows; flip animation leaves row in a
  half state if clicked mid-transition.
- A11y: rows are buttons or have role, both faces reachable by screen reader, not
  hover-only.

**8. Playground filters (segment + question)**
- [ ] Changing the segment chip re-renders the chart with that segment's data.
- [ ] Changing the question/section re-renders without losing the selected segment (and
      vice versa) — the two filters must not reset each other.
- [ ] Index colouring is correct: ≥120 over-indexes, ≤80 under-indexes.
- [ ] Renders only from `data-src/cocl_crosstab.json`; no hand-typed numbers; Q2 label bug
      fix applied (don't render the mislabelled "Optimistic about next decade" row).
- [ ] Empty/awaiting-data states (e.g. segment-level figures not in file) show a clear
      "not available" message, not a blank or a zero.
- Common breakage: selecting a question resets segment to "all" (Sequential Undo across the
  two filters); chart caches first render and ignores filter change; relabel mapping
  (Adapters→Architects etc.) not applied so chips show wrong names.
- A11y: chips/selects are labelled controls, chart has a text/table fallback, change
  announced.

### Cross-cutting smooth-path checks
- [ ] `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on every animated path (scroll reveal, counters,
      flips).
- [ ] No control is hover-only without a click/tap equivalent.
- [ ] Every control has a visible focus state and is reachable by Tab in logical order.
- [ ] After any interaction, the on-screen result matches what the control's label promised.

## D) FRONTEND TOP-TIER CHECKLIST

Make it feel premium and intentional, tuned to the VCCP brand system: **mustard primary,
square corners (no border-radius), no gradients, Inter Tight, highlighter-parallelogram
motif, teal accents for shifts/research surfaces.** The goal is refined, editorial,
confident — not maximalist. Elegance comes from executing restraint well.

### 1. Hierarchy (the highest-leverage lever)
- [ ] One clear focal point per viewport: the headline, or the hero stat, or the chart.
      Never two things shouting.
- [ ] Type scale is deliberate and limited (e.g. kicker / headline / standfirst / body /
      caption — 4–5 steps, not ad-hoc sizes). Big jump between headline and body.
- [ ] The highlighter word is the one emphasis per headline; don't also bold, colour and
      underline elsewhere in the same line.
- [ ] Stat numbers are the largest type on a data surface; labels are quiet and secondary.

### 2. Spacing rhythm
- [ ] Spacing comes from a consistent scale (e.g. 8px base: 8/16/24/40/64/96). No random
      17px, 23px values.
- [ ] Generous, consistent vertical rhythm between sections; each chapter breathes.
- [ ] Align to a grid; left edges of kicker/headline/standfirst/body share a baseline.
- [ ] Whitespace is intentional — give the hero stat room; don't fill every gap.

### 3. Motion restraint
- [ ] One well-orchestrated reveal per section (staggered entrance) beats scattered
      micro-animations. Reveal, then rest.
- [ ] Easing is smooth (ease-out for entrances), durations 200–500ms; nothing bouncy or slow.
- [ ] Counters count once on enter-view, not on every scroll.
- [ ] Motion is square-brand-appropriate: no parallax circus, no gratuitous parallax/3D.
- [ ] `prefers-reduced-motion` disables non-essential motion.

### 4. Density vs whitespace
- [ ] Editorial chapters (cover, twists, outro): low density, lots of air, big type.
- [ ] Data chapters (baselines, segments, playground): higher density is fine, but use
      consistent gutters and align numbers in tabular columns (tabular-nums).
- [ ] Tables/charts: align numbers right or on the decimal; labels left; no centre-justified
      number columns.
- [ ] Don't centre long-form text; left-align body copy for readability.

### 5. Focus states & interactive polish
- [ ] Every interactive element has a visible, on-brand focus ring (mustard or teal, square,
      offset) — never `outline: none` with no replacement.
- [ ] Hover states are crisp and instant-feeling; selected/active states are unmistakable
      and not colour-only (add weight, border, or fill).
- [ ] Buttons feel like buttons: clear hit area (min 44px touch target), state on
      hover/active/focus/disabled.
- [ ] Cursor is `pointer` on anything clickable.

### 6. Brand-fidelity guardrails (VCCP)
- [ ] No border-radius anywhere — corners stay square.
- [ ] No gradients — flat fills only (mustard, teal, black, off-white, grey).
- [ ] Inter Tight throughout; weights do the hierarchy work.
- [ ] Mustard is the primary accent; teal (#00BCA5 up-shift / #80E8E3 surface) for
      research/shift contexts; mustard-dark (#FF8812) for down-shifts.
- [ ] Girl-and-Bear / logo: black silhouette, never on mustard, never animated or distorted.
- [ ] No pie/donut charts — bars, waffle, tabular numbers only.

### 7. Premium finish details
- [ ] Consistent footnote/source line on every data surface (standard line from STORY.md).
- [ ] Optical alignment on the highlighter parallelogram (sits on the word, not floating).
- [ ] Crisp 1px rules/dividers, consistent and sparing.
- [ ] Images/charts render sharp on retina; SVGs not raster where possible.
- [ ] No layout shift on load; reserve space for charts and counters.
