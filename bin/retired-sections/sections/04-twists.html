Beneath the expected cutbacks, three anomalies emerge — a population not retreating into a bunker, but rewriting the rules of survival, institutional trust and technology.

  1. 01 Trust has shifted, not collapsed
  2. 02 Joy is ring-fenced, not abandoned
  3. 03 Expertise is a prompt away

Twist 01

Britain’s trust has shifted, not collapsed.

Just one institution of seven clears half the country’s confidence — and government trails every other. Before we show you: rank them as you think Britain does, most trusted at the top.

A 29-point spread in who Britain trusts to be there

53% NHS — the most trusted → 24% Government — the least

And the paradox: the NHS scores 6.42 / 10, the highest trust of any institution — yet 53% say its performance has declined this decade. High regard and falling confidence, side by side.

Twist 02

The holiday is sacrosanct.

Hospitality and fashion are vulnerable. But the holiday has been reclassified as a wellbeing line to defend, not a luxury to trim. Try to drag it out of the budget — it won’t go.

40% protect their holiday budget at all costs — the single most-defended non-essential in the country.

Twist 03

Expertise is now a prompt away.

Advice you once paid a professional for — finance, health, legal — is being substituted. See how far it goes for everyday tasks, and for the high-stakes calls.

I’ve used ChatGPT to look at pensions and tax rules and tax savings and self-assessment tax regulations and investment opportunities and some things for my son when he was buying a house — and by and large, it was pretty good.
